\section{Quantum Fermi Gas}
Before we discuss neutron stars as fermi gas, we must discuss fermi gas. The formalism developed in this section serves two purposes: it provides the equation of state relating pressure to energy density in the neutron star interior, and it gives us the tools needed to assess whether the zero-temperature (cold gas) approximation is justified at the densities found within these objects.

We will pick up where Oppenheimer and Volkoff started and consider a pure neutron gas. because neutrons are fermions (spin-$\frac{1}{2}$), they obey fermi-statistics. That is to say that their distribution function is Fermi-Dirac:
\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:distfunc}
    f(E) = \frac{1}{\exp[\beta(E-\mu)]+1}
\end{equation}
Where $\beta = 1/\kappa_B T$, $\kappa_B$ the boltzmann constant and $T$ the temperature, $E$ is the energy of a given state, and $\mu$ is the chemical potential, given that we have some fixed $N$ number of particles.
